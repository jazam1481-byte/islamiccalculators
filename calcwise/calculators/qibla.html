What is the Qibla?

The Qibla (قِبْلَة) is the direction Muslims face during Salah (prayer). It points toward the Masjid al-Haram in Makkah, Saudi Arabia, and specifically the Kaaba — the sacred cube-shaped structure at its center. All Muslims worldwide face this same direction in their prayers, creating a symbolic unity of the Ummah.

How is Qibla direction calculated?

The Qibla bearing is calculated using the Great Circle method — the shortest path along the Earth's curved surface between your location and the Kaaba (21.4225°N, 39.8262°E). This gives the most accurate compass bearing for prayer.

Note: For locations directly opposite Makkah on the globe, both directions are equidistant — in such cases, consult a local imam.
