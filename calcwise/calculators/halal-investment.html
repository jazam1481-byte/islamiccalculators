Shariah-Compliant Investment Options

  • Halal ETFs — Wahed Invest, Amundi Islamic, SP Funds Dow Jones Global Sukuk ETF
  • Sukuk — Islamic bonds backed by real assets, traded on global exchanges
  • Islamic equity funds — Screened to exclude alcohol, tobacco, weapons, financials with riba
  • Islamic REITs — Real estate investment trusts operating on Ijarah structures
  • Gold — Physical or Shariah-compliant gold ETFs

Shariah Screening Criteria

For a stock or fund to be halal, it must pass both business activity screening (no prohibited industries) and financial ratio screening (debt-to-market-cap typically below 33%, interest income below 5% of total revenue). Several screening services exist including MSCI Islamic Index, Dow Jones Islamic Market Index, and AAOIFI standards.
